What is QGenesis?

The Challenge

NASA has accumulated decades of bioscience research from space experiments, but finding connections between studies is difficult. Traditional search relies on keywords and simple similarity—missing deeper, hidden relationships that could unlock breakthrough discoveries for Moon and Mars missions.

Our Solution

QGenesis combines classical AI with quantum computing to create a next-generation knowledge engine. We use AI embeddings for intelligent search and summarization, then leverage quantum algorithms to re-rank results—surfacing more relevant and diverse papers by uncovering non-obvious connections.

How It Works

AI Layer

Advanced AI models process NASA's papers, creating semantic embeddings that capture the meaning and context of each study. Natural language queries return the most relevant initial results.

Quantum Layer

Quantum kernel methods running on IBM's quantum computers analyze the top candidates, computing quantum similarity scores that reveal subtle, non-linear relationships classical systems miss.

Future-Ready

As quantum hardware matures, QGenesis will scale to process larger datasets and uncover even more complex patterns—paving the way for accelerated scientific discovery in space biology.

Current Focus

We're building a proof-of-concept using NASA's Open Science Data Repository (OSDR), focusing on microgravity effects on mouse musculoskeletal systems. This smaller-scale demonstration validates our quantum-enhanced approach and showcases its potential for future space biology research.
